A national innovation agency has launched a competitive research grant program to support innovative solutions that address real-world challenges using Computing and Information Technology. The agency is seeking proposals that not only investigate problems but also design, develop, evaluate, and communicate innovative technological artifacts such as software systems, mobile applications, intelligent systems, cloud-based solutions, IoT platforms, cybersecurity tools, machine learning models, or decision support systems.
As a research team, you have been invited to compete for the grant. To qualify, your team must prepare a professional research proposal that follows academic research standards and adopts the Design Science Research (DSR) methodology, which emphasizes the creation and evaluation of technological solutions to solve identified problems.
Your proposal will undergo academic review before approval. Therefore, it should demonstrate a well-defined research problem, clear objectives, an appropriate research methodology, ethical considerations, and a feasible implementation plan.

Task 6: Chapter Three – Research Methodology Using Design Science Research (20 Marks)

Develop Chapter Three by adopting the Design Science Research (DSR) methodology.

Your methodology should explain how the proposed technological artifact will be designed, developed, demonstrated, evaluated, and communicated.

Include the following sections:

3.1 Introduction

Provide an overview of the research methodology.

3.2 Research Design

Explain why Design Science Research (DSR) is appropriate for the study.
Describe how DSR focuses on creating innovative technological artifacts to solve practical problems rather than only observing or explaining phenomena.

3.3 Design Science Research Process

Describe how the study will follow the Design Science Research lifecycle, including:
•	Problem Identification and Motivation

•	Definition of Research Objectives

•	Design of the Proposed Artifact

•	Development of the Artifact

•	Demonstration of the Artifact

•	Evaluation of the Artifact

•	Communication of Research Findings

Illustrate the DSR process using a suitable framework or diagram.
3.4 Study Area

Describe where the research will be conducted.
3.5 Target Users or Participants

Identify the intended users, stakeholders, or participants involved in the study.
3.6 Sampling Strategy

Describe the sampling method and sample size used for collecting evaluation data.
3.7 Data Collection Methods

Explain the techniques that will be used, such as:
•	Questionnaires

•	Interviews

•	Observations

•	Focus Group Discussions

•	System-generated logs

•	Expert reviews

3.8 Artifact Design and Development Tools

Describe the technologies and tools that will be used to develop the proposed solution, for example:
•	Programming languages

•	Frameworks

•	Databases

•	Cloud platforms

•	AI or Machine Learning libraries

•	Development environments

•	Version control systems

3.9 Artifact Evaluation

Describe how the developed solution will be evaluated.
Possible evaluation techniques include:

•	Functional testing

•	Usability testing

•	Performance evaluation

•	Security testing

•	Accuracy 

•	User Acceptance Testing (UAT)

•	Expert evaluation

•	Comparative evaluation with existing solutions

Clearly define the evaluation metrics that will be used.
3.10 Data Analysis

Explain how quantitative and qualitative data collected during evaluation will be analyzed.
3.11 Ethical Considerations

Describe how research ethics, confidentiality, privacy, informed consent, and data protection will be maintained.
3.12 Work Plan

Prepare a project timeline or Gantt chart.
3.13 Budget

Estimate the resources required to complete the research project.

Learning Outcomes
Upon successful completion of this assignment, students should be able to:
1.	Formulate a researchable problem and develop an innovative research topic in Computing or Information Technology.
2.	Prepare a professional research proposal following accepted academic standards.
3.	Critically review literature to identify research gaps and justify the need for a technological solution.
4.	Apply the Design Science Research (DSR) methodology to design, develop, demonstrate, and evaluate a technology-based artifact.
5.	Select appropriate data collection, analysis, and evaluation methods for validating the proposed solution.
6.	Demonstrate ethical research practices and effective academic communication.
7.	Produce a proposal suitable for institutional review and implementation as a technology-driven research project.
